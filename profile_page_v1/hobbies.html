Underwater Hockey

Underwater hockey, also known as Octopush is a globally played limited-contact sport in which two teams compete to manoeuvre a puck across the bottom of a swimming pool into the opposing team's goal by propelling it with a hockey stick. The sport is recognized by the International Olympic Committee and is governed by the World Underwater Federation (CMAS). It is now played in more than 40 countries, with an estimated 15,000 participants. I have been playing this thrilling sport for 6 years so far and have travelled across Australia to different tournaments (the next one is in Gold Coast).
